6. Express yourself

Communicating and speaking up is a powerful and important skill to have. Here are some pointers on how, when, and why to use it.

Let's Think!

Journal-writing provides an excellent outlet for emotions. What other benefits do you think writing in a journal has? How often do you write in your journal?

20

Reduces stress—expressing your feelings with friends and family reduces stress and induces feelings of being loved. Facing your fears reduces stress since the experience is (usually) never as bad as we imagine.

Healthier relationships—speaking your mind, whether it is positive or not, helps build relationships since it requires honesty and trust. It makes one more empathetic and reduces chances of miscommunication.

Self-confidence—over coming stage fright, shyness, or anxiety, and breaking unhealthy self-belief increases self confidence in leaps and bounds.

Introspection usually follows speaking. Putting thoughts and feelings into words gives us an opportunity to understand ourselves and look inwards.

Bravery—discussing a stigmatized issue, talking about difficult situations, standing up for something you believe in, or deciding to get out of your comfort zone—they all require and instil a great sense of bravery which is an invaluable life skill.

Believe it or not, but speaking out helps you improve your vocabulary, whether it's speaking with friends and family, listening to music, writing in your journal, speaking at a debate, or standing up for what you believe in.
