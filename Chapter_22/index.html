22. Money, money, money

A bank is an institution that deals in money. When individuals have money, they put this money in a bank. This is called a deposit. When individuals need money, they borrow it from a bank. This is called a loan.

Banks create money in the economy by lending some of the deposited money as loans and charging a fee called interest.

The percentage of deposits that a bank can lend in the form of loans is decided by the Reserve Bank of India.

The Reserve Bank of India plays a crucial role in the economy. It issues new currency notes, manages the Government’s money, creates the country’s monetary policy, regulates the banking system, and manages foreign reserves.

If you look at any currency note, you will notice that it contains the signature of the Governor of the Reserve Bank of India at the time the note was printed.

In 2016, the Reserve Bank of India declared the 500 and 1000 rupees notes in circulation as invalid. This is called demonetisation. New ` 500 notes and ` 2000 note were printed and brought into circulation. This was the third time India had faced demonetisation.

The first demonetisation was in 1946 when the currency notes of ` 1,000 and ` 10,000 were removed from circulation. However, they were reintroduced in 1958. The second demonetisation happened in 1978 when ` 1000, ` 5000, and ` 10,000 were put out of circulation.
