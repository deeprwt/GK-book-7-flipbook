16. Music euphony

Let’s read about different styles of music from around the world.

Cuba: Mambo originated in Cuba in the 1940s and typically features rhythmic sounds created by the congas, cajon, banjo, and timbales. Tito Puente and Machito are famous Mambo musicians.

Brazil: Bossa Nova developed in Brazil over the 1950s and 1960s. The term means ‘new trend’ in Portuguese. It is a fusion of samba and jazz. Prominent artists are Astrud Gilberto and Stan Getz.

New Orleans: Jazz music is characterised by rhythmic sounds using the drums, trumpet, piano, bass and other instruments. It is a synthesis of Western music and African beats. Louis Armstrong and Charlie Parker are leading musicians of this genre.

Chile: Cueca is style of dance and music that features the guitar, accordion, and trumpet. Los Chileneros and Tito Fernandez are famous Cueca artists.

52

Italy: Opera is an art form which involves the telling of a story through singing and dramatic performance. It is characterised by the female performer singing in high-pitched notes, and the male performer singing in low-pitched notes, while accompanied by classical music. Luciano Pavarotti and Maria Callas are notable opera performers.

Europe: Western Classical music is instrumental in nature and uses harp, piano, violin, cello, and flute. Mozart and Beethoven are prominent Western Classical music composers.

USA: Hip-hop combines rhythmic music with rapping. It originated in the 1970s in New York’s Bronx area. Tupac Shakur and Jay-Z are prominent artists of this genre.

North Africa: Berber music is the traditional music of the nomadic tribe of North Africa. Singers narrate tales of nomadic or village life to music from the tambourine, traditional bagpipes, drums, and the flute. Slimane Azem and Houria Aichi are popular Berber musicians.

India: Carnatic music evolved in Southern India and is associated with temple life. It involves a group of musicians playing instruments like the mridangam, violin, and the veena while one main singer usually sings along. MS Subbalakshmi and KJ Yesudas are prominent Carnatic artists.
