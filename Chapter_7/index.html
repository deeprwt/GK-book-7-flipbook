Global warming refers to the increase in the temperature of the earth.

CAUSES

• Green house emissions which create holes in the Earth's ozone layer and trap the Earth's heat cause temperatures to increase at a faster rate. The major green house gases are carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide.

• The world's top three carbon dioxide emitters produce more than 50% of the world's carbon emissions. They are: 1. China-28.21%, 2. USA-15.99%, 3. India-6.24%

• Rampant deforestation has been a source of concern since trees absorb carbon dioxide and keep the earth cool. According to the World Wildlife Fund, an estimated 15% of all green house gas emissions are a result of deforestation.

The causes of deforestation include small-sector agriculture, commercial agriculture, logging, and clearing of forest land for human settlements.

EFFECTS

Climate change has led to increased temperatures, rising sea levels, melting ice caps, increased famines and droughts, and spread of diseases, just to name a few.

• 16 of the 17 warmest years have occurred between 2001 and now. 90% of the 10 hottest years in history occurred between 2005 and 2016. 2016 was the hottest year in history and the third consecutive hottest year on record. The graph shows the decade by decade increase in temperature, and the hottest years in history.

• Melting ice caps have led to rising sea levels and sea temperatures. This has caused the loss of marine life and the displacement of many coastal communities. 50 per cent of the Great Barrier Reef is believed to be dead because of rising sea temperatures. Antarctica has been losing an average of 134 billion metric tons of ice every year.

• Climate change has led to increasing droughts, resulting in famines and facilitating the spread of diseases like cholera and malaria.

• Weather disturbances have increased. The change in climate has resulted in extremely destructive storms like Hurricane Harvey that devastated Texas in the US, in 2017. In 2015, a record 22 hurricanes or typhoons of high-strength hit the Northern Hemisphere. In Western USA, forest fires have increased nine fold between 1984 and 2015 due to rising temperatures. In Australia, three of the five worst wildfires have occurred between 2003 and 2015. Over 30 major wildfires have occurred there since 2000.
